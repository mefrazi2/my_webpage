Projects

My approach to creating impactful maps is understanding it is both an art and a science. Visualizing data in compeling and clear ways is an important step in map-making. When applicable, I automated processes by creating scripts using Python.

While creating visulizations in the form of maps, I also support GIS and data literacy by delivering workshops that focus on the fundamentals of both proprietary and open-source GIS applications.

1 | cold war | In collaboration with faculty at Northwestern University, this map depicts the Berlin Wall during the Cold War in 1970.

3 | tornadoes | Projections are a really useful tool when visualizing maps, as is adding additional graphics like simple graphs to gain a better sense of distribution.

5 | ebk analysis | I'm interested in using interpoability methods to better understand continuous data and how it can improve our understanding of missing data.

7 | palmer penguins | I enjoy working in R for simple data visualizations that you just can't customize in other applications. It's also sometimes useful for making maps, although I prefer GIS software over R.

2 | annual rainfall | Zonal statistics is a great tool for manipulating raster imagery in a simple way. This map illustrates annual rainfall for Guatemala.

4 | geopuzzle challenge | Each year, Northwestern hosts GeoWeek in November. This visualization dipicts the range of times for each puzzle type from the '21 GeoPuzzle Challenge.

6 | vaccines | As part of a project I'm working on to examine the relationship between political ideology and vaccine hesitancy using ZIP code data.
